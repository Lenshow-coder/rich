Goal: 
determine if bad results are due to bad luck, sample size, or running bad on big vs small bets
compare estimated edge vs actual edge (ROI) in buckets


Plan:
break down by odds bands
break down by spreads, MLs, totals
break down by edge

merge correlated bets and break down by bet size


Key takeaways:
Expected edge is greatly overstated for big underdogs (>+150) and for bets where expected edge is >10%.
Moneylines are outperforming spreads and totals by a large margin.
You are indeed running bad on big vs small bets - you are roughly breaking even on bets over $1000, but making close to the expected profit on bets under $1000.

Other notes:
Sample size is not high enough for definitive actions (ex. not betting spreads and totals), but the takeaways are still useful to keep in mind (ex. don't size bets that show a 15% edge as if that's the true edge).
